Medium Level

Time to level up your game!

Ready to add some spice to your Sudoku life? These techniques will make those numbers dance to your tune:

1. Pairs and Triplets (Number Buddies)

Sometimes two cells in the same row, column, or box can only contain the same two numbers (like 4 and 7). These are called 'pairs' - they're like best friends who won't let any other numbers sit with them. This means those two numbers can't appear anywhere else in that row, column, or box!

Example: If two cells in a row can only be 4 or 7, and you find a third cell that could be 4, 7, or 9 - guess what? It must be 9!

2. Box/Line Reduction (The Line Dance)

When a number can only appear in two or three cells within a box, AND those cells all line up in the same row or column, that number can't appear anywhere else in that row or column outside the box.

Think of it like this: if a box has three possible spots for a 6, all in the same row, then that 6 HAS to be in one of those spots. Therefore, 6 can't appear anywhere else in that row outside the box!
