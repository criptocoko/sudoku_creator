Extreme Level

Push your limits

These techniques separate the Sudoku masters from the merely talented:

Swordfish (The Triple Threat)

Like X-Wing but with three rows and three columns. When you find a number that can only appear in 2-3 cells in each of three different rows, and these cells line up in three columns, you've found a Swordfish! This means the number must appear in three of these nine cells, one in each row.

XY-Wing (The Three Amigos)

Find three cells: one with options AB, another with BC, and a third with AC (where A, B, and C are different numbers). If they're connected properly, you can eliminate C from any cells that see both the AC and BC cells!
