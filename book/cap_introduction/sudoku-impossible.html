Impossible Level

The ultimate challenge

Welcome to techniques so advanced, even some Sudoku masters scratch their heads:

Alternating Inference Chains (The Domino Effect)

Imagine a chain of cells where each cell is connected to the next by a shared possible number. If you can make the chain return to the start, you can make some powerful deductions! It's like playing connect-the-dots, but with numbers, and in your mind.

Nishio (The Leap of Faith)

When all else fails, try this: Pick a cell with only two possibilities, assume one is correct, and follow the logic. If you hit a contradiction, your assumption was wrong - use the other number! It's like being a detective who tests theories to solve the crime.

Remember: At this level, it's perfectly normal to stare at the puzzle for so long that your family stages an intervention. Just explain to them that you're not obsessed - you're dedicated to science!
